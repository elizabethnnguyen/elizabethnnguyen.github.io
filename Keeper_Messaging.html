Keeper: P5, Atom 2020.

A mini-game that loosely imitates online chatting. as soon as a user signs onto their computer, they are met with some messages left by their friends and family (and in this case, Mom, Jenn, and Kev). The user actually doesn't see the messages as the program has a Keeper that keeps track of the user's messages without having to go through the fuss of going from one text to another. It's like a digital voicemail box but with texts. The Keeper guides the user throughout the experience, asking what they would like to say or do as their response. The user basically talks to the Keeper directly, while also responding to their friends and family indirectly. Each member in the buddy list also has viewable profiles, but these are only activated when the Keeper is actually discussing about this person. This whole thing goes on until the very end when the Keeper says that there are no more messages, thus it signs out and the user will then be prompted to restart if they would like.

(warning: be cautious of volume!)
